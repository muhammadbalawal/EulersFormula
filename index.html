Euler's Formula

Euler's formula is expressed as:
\( e^{i\theta} = \cos(\theta) + i\sin(\theta) \).

As we increase the angle \( \theta \):

  • Cosine: Controls horizontal position on the circle. As \( \theta \) increases, \( \cos(\theta) \) moves from 1 to -1 along the X-axis.
  • Sine: Controls vertical position. As \( \theta \) increases, \( \sin(\theta) \) moves from 0 to 1 to -1 along the Y-axis.
  • \( e^{i\theta} \): A rotating point combining both \( \cos(\theta) \) and \( \sin(\theta) \), tracing a circle as \( \theta \) increases.

At \( e^{i\pi} \), when \( \theta \) reaches 180Â°, the point hits -1 on the real axis. This is Euler's Identity!

So, as \( \theta \) increases, the complex number rotates around the circle, with \( \cos(\theta) \) moving left/right and \( \sin(\theta) \) moving up/down.
